10-minute word-for-word script for tomorrow

Use this page as your speaking teleprompter: 7 min narrative + 2 min live demo + 1 min ask. Includes the exact wording for "real photo -> model conversion" and source attribution.

Audience: judges + potential customers Style: clear, technical, confident Core proof: benchmark uplift

Full Verbatim Timeline

0:00-0:40 · Opening

Problem + one-line value

Say: "Manual trap placement on farms is still experience-heavy, hard to standardise, and difficult to audit. BioPath converts a farm layout into an optimised trap plan with measurable uplift, not guesswork."

中文提示：先讲痛点，再讲一句话价值，不要先讲算法。

0:40-1:40 · Real-world context

Explain real photo and source

Say: "This photo is Cambridge University Farm from a public Wikimedia source. We use public imagery only as contextual grounding, not as private operational telemetry."

Say: "For reproducible optimisation, we transform context into a synthetic walkable geometry plus a transparent, photo-informed prior distribution."

1:40-3:10 · Conversion chain

How real photo becomes a computable map

Say: "The conversion has three explicit steps: first, real-world context photo; second, geometry extraction into a grid network; third, a risk-prior field that represents plausible rodent activity intensity."

Say: "Every assumption is explicit in JSON, so reviewers can inspect and rerun exactly the same pipeline."

Key line: "It is transparent and repeatable, not a black box."

3:10-4:40 · Technical originality

Research-backed implementation

Say: "Our stack combines graph-based reachability features, stochastic movement modelling, and Monte Carlo validation. We optimise placement under a fixed trap budget and evaluate robustness across movement scenarios."

Say: "This is where my research background on stochastic processes is directly translated into an operational decision-support tool."

4:40-5:50 · Show result heatmap

Interpreting the demo heatmap

Say: "This heatmap shows distance to nearest trap. Darker regions are already close to coverage; brighter regions indicate potential blind areas. The red crosses are optimized trap coordinates."

Say: "Because this run uses a photo-informed distribution, we are optimising against a more realistic activity prior than uniform assumptions."

5:50-7:00 · Funding + credibility

Ceres/LINCAM and Cambridge track

Say: "We are simultaneously pursuing Cambridge farm funding pathways. In parallel, we align with Ceres AgriTech where the roadshow material states that Ceres delivers the LINCAM translational research funding programme, with up to £150K for eligible projects."

Say: "Our goal is to convert this into real farm pilot evidence over an 8-week cycle."

7:00-9:00 · Live demo

Two-minute operational proof

Say while clicking: "I now run solve and benchmark in the same UI workspace. We get trap coordinates, capture probability, robust score, and uplift versus random baseline in one pass."

Say: "This creates decision evidence we can compare across farms and across time."

9:00-10:00 · Ask

Close with explicit ask

Say: "We ask for two pilot introductions, a small PoC budget, and practical farm data access for an 8-week trial. In return, we deliver run-level uplift evidence and a clear go/no-go scaling recommendation."

中文提示：最后10秒重复一次 ask，不要加新信息。

Q&A fallback lines (high probability questions)

  • Is the heatmap "real"? "The photo is real and publicly sourced. The optimisation map is a transparent synthetic geometry with explicit priors for reproducible benchmarking."
  • Why not full satellite/CV pipeline now? "Today we prioritised repeatable decision evidence. The next pilot stage is exactly where richer site data will be integrated."
  • What is the biggest technical edge? "Robust trap placement under stochastic movement uncertainty, validated by Monte Carlo rather than single-path assumptions."
