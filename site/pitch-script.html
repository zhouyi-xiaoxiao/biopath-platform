10-minute speaker script built for clarity first

This is a word-for-word flow: clear opening, concrete example, measurable proof, practical funding ask. Structure: 7 min narrative + 2 min live demo + 1 min ask.

Audience: judges + potential customers Tone: clear, concise, concrete Timing: 10:00 exact (600s) Core proof: benchmark uplift

Official judging criteria reference

Source: Basecamp Outlook email "Week 5 - Resources and Partners and information about pitching" (12 Feb 2026).

  1. Clarity of communicating idea
  2. Level of market and customer knowledge and understanding
  3. Degree of understanding and appropriateness of chosen business model
  4. Logic of pitch deck order and quality of presentation skills
  5. Robustness and reality of finance

Scoring scale: each criterion marked out of 5 (5 Excellent, 4 Good, 3 Acceptable, 2 needs more depth/detail, 1 poor or insufficient detail).

Full 10-minute verbatim timeline

Timing check: all segments sum to exactly 10:00 (600 seconds).

0:00-0:50 · Opening · Criterion 1

Two-sentence definition (investor version)

Say: "BioPath turns a farm map into optimized trap coordinates with measurable uplift, so placement is evidence-based rather than guesswork."

Say: "Our model is pilot-to-subscription decision software for farms and pest-control operators."

中文提示：开场不要讲算法，先两句话讲清“做什么 + 怎么赚钱”。

0:50-1:40 · Concrete example · Criteria 1 + 2

One specific scene, not abstract language

Say: "In a Cambridge-style layout, operators repeatedly miss one feed-corridor and one storage-edge bottleneck under manual placement. We run BioPath and return six specific trap points plus benchmark uplift in one workflow."

Key line: "Specific scene first, then method."

1:40-2:40 · Real context + source · Criteria 1 + 2

Explain the real-photo conversion transparently

Say: "This image is Cambridge University Farm from a public Wikimedia source. We use public imagery only as contextual grounding, not private telemetry."

Say: "For reproducibility, we convert context into synthetic walkable geometry and a transparent photo-informed prior distribution."

2:40-3:50 · Product + method · Criteria 1 + 4

Keep method understandable

Say: "Pipeline is map to graph, stochastic movement simulation, optimization under fixed trap budget, then Monte Carlo benchmark against random baseline."

Say: "The point is not fancy visuals. The point is reproducible, auditable decisions."

3:50-5:10 · Traction speed · Criteria 2 + 4

Execution velocity, with dates

Say: "In the last cycle, we shipped solver CLI, benchmark API, online pitch studio, and run-level artifacts for review. That velocity is the traction signal at this stage."

Say: "Our latest demo evidence reports capture probability, robust score, and uplift versus random in one run."

5:10-6:20 · Market + business model · Criteria 2 + 3

Bottom-up market logic

Say: "We start with pilot customers, price per site per year, and convert through operator portfolios. This is a bottom-up path, not a top-down market-size slogan."

Say: "Business model is paid pilot first, then recurring decision-support subscription with service layer."

6:20-7:00 · Funding reality · Criteria 3 + 5

Use practical funding band

Say: "For the next step, we target LINCAM Proof of Concept Awards, officially listed at £5,000 to £20,000 with a 7-minute pitch format."

Say: "That funding range matches our immediate pilot-evidence milestones."

7:00-9:00 · Live demo · Criteria 1 + 4

Two-minute operational proof

Say while clicking: "Now I run solve and benchmark in one interface. You can see trap coordinates, capture probability, robust score, and uplift versus random baseline."

Say: "This output is what teams can act on immediately and what reviewers can audit."

Ask audience: "Is this clear so far, or should I show one more concrete map example?"

9:00-10:00 · Ask · Criteria 3 + 5

Explicit ask tied to 18-24 month outcomes

Say: "We ask for two pilot introductions, practical data access for one 8-week trial, and support for a LINCAM PoC application in the £5k–£20k range."

Say: "In return, we deliver run-level uplift evidence, first recurring customer conversions, and a funding-ready scale package over the next 18 to 24 months."

中文提示：最后一句一定要是“钱换里程碑”，不要只说“钱要拿来招人”。

High-probability Q&A lines

  • "Is the heatmap real?" "The photo context is real and public. The optimization space is a transparent synthetic geometry for reproducible benchmarking."
  • "Why not full CV/satellite now?" "At this stage we prioritize repeatable decisions and pilot evidence; richer site data integration is next-stage work."
  • "Why should we trust this team?" "Because the same team built the solver, API, benchmark, and deployment loop, and we can show run-level evidence end-to-end."
